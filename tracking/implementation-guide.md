# Implementation Guide — 12-Week Recomposition
**Adherence, Monitoring, and Plateau Protocols**

---

## Week-by-Week Execution Checklist

### Before Week 1 Starts
- [ ] Weigh yourself Monday morning (post-toilet, pre-food, same scale every time)
- [ ] Take photos: front, side, back (same lighting, same time of day)
- [ ] Measure: waist (at navel), chest, hips, arms (flexed), thighs
- [ ] Establish baseline lifts: test 5RM on bench, squat, deadlift, OHP in Week 1
- [ ] Download a calorie tracking app (Cronometer or MyFitnessPal — Cronometer is more accurate for Indian foods)
- [ ] Set up your logbook or notes app for tracking weights and reps
- [ ] Buy creatine and start loading (no load needed — just 5g/day from Day 1)
- [ ] Stock fish market, butcher, and pantry as per shopping guide

---

## Body Composition Monitoring

### What to Track Weekly (Every Monday)

| Metric | Method | Target Trend |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Bodyweight | Scale, morning, post-toilet | Down 0.5–1 kg/week average |
| Waist circumference | Tape at navel, relaxed | Down 0.5–1 cm every 2 weeks |
| Strength (main lifts) | Logbook | Flat or up — NEVER should drop >5% |
| Energy levels | 1–10 self-rating | Should stay ≥7 |
| Sleep quality | 1–10 self-rating | Target ≥7 |

### Monthly Measurements (Every 4 Weeks = Deload Week)
- Weight, all circumferences, photos
- This is your real progress check — weekly fluctuations are water/food weight

### What Good Progress Looks Like

**Weeks 1–4:**
- Weight: −1 to −3 kg total
- Strength: flat (establishing baseline) to slight increase
- Waist: −1 to −2 cm

**Weeks 5–8:**
- Weight: −2 to −4 kg total over the phase (−3 to −7 kg from start)
- Strength: holding or increasing on compounds
- Waist: −2 to −4 cm from start

**Weeks 9–12:**
- Weight: −2 to −3 kg total over the phase (−5 to −10 kg from start)
- Strength: should be hitting or near personal records despite deficit
- Waist: −4 to −6 cm from start (or more)

**Realistic 12-week expectation:** −5 to −8 kg scale weight, −3 to −5% body fat, visible muscle gain (particularly if starting BF is 18–25%). At 20 years training, this is excellent and sustainable recomposition.

## Weekly Check-In Protocol

Every Sunday night, answer these 5 questions:

1. **Did I hit my training sessions?** (5/5, 4/5, etc.)
2. **Did I stay within 100 kcal of my calorie target on 5+ days?**
3. **Did I hit 200g+ protein every day?**
4. **Did I sleep 7+ hours on 5+ nights?**
5. **Am I showing downward trend in waist or weight?**

If you score ≥4/5: stay the course.
If you score 3/5: identify the one weak area and fix it next week.
If you score ≤2/5: do not increase deficit or cardio — fix adherence first.

## Plateau Protocols

### Scenario 1 — Weight Has Not Moved for 2 Weeks

**Diagnosis first:** Is your tracking accurate?
- Are you weighing your food or estimating? Estimation is almost always 20–40% low.
- Are you logging cooking oil? 1 tbsp coconut oil = 120 kcal and is easy to miss.
- Are you counting drinks? Coconut water (local Goa = 60–100 kcal per glass), chai with milk, etc.

**If tracking is accurate and weight still stalled:**
1. Add 15 min LISS to one session this week only
2. Drop carbs by 25g on 2 rest days (but not training days)
3. Wait 10 days before making another change

**Do NOT do all of the above simultaneously.** One variable at a time.

---

### Scenario 2 — Strength Dropping Significantly (>5% Over 2 Sessions)

This is a signal that your deficit is too aggressive or recovery is insufficient.

**Immediate actions:**
1. Add 200 kcal from carbs on training days (1 extra cup of cooked rice)
2. Reduce HIIT from 20 min to 10 min for 2 weeks
3. Prioritize sleep — non-negotiable 7.5+ hours
4. Check if you're eating within 60 minutes post-workout

**If strength continues falling after 2 weeks on above fixes:**
- Take a 1-week diet break at maintenance calories (3,100 kcal)
- Train normally, no cardio beyond LISS Saturday
- Then return to Phase 2 deficit (not Phase 3 even if you're in week 9)

---

### Scenario 3 — Fat Loss Stalls but Waist Is Still Shrinking

This is fine — you're likely gaining muscle as you lose fat (recomposition). Scale weight can be flat for 3–4 weeks while body composition changes significantly.

**Do not increase deficit.** Continue as planned. Trust the mirror and tape over the scale.

---

### Scenario 4 — Extreme Hunger / Cravings (Adherence Breaking)

**Causes and fixes:**

| Cause | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Insufficient food volume | Shift calories to high-volume low-calorie foods (more vegetables, soups) |
| Not enough protein at breakfast | Add 2 more egg whites to Meal 1 |
| Missing a meal | Use a timed alarm for every meal for first 3 weeks |
| Social eating in Goa (restaurants, bars) | Pre-plan: grilled fish + salad at any Goan restaurant = safe |
| Alcohol (common in Goa social scene) | Hard limit: 2 drinks/week max, skip the food pairing, avoid beer (200+ kcal/500ml) |

---

### Scenario 5 — Joint Pain or Overuse Injury

With 20 years training in a caloric deficit, joint stress accumulates. Be proactive:

**Prevention:**
- Warm up thoroughly (10 min on treadmill light walk + dynamic stretching before every session)
- Never skip the deload weeks — this is when tendons recover
- Fish oil supplementation directly supports joint health

**If pain appears:**
- Swap the painful movement for a pain-free alternative (e.g., knee pain → replace back squat with hack squat or leg press temporarily)
- Do not train through sharp or worsening pain
- Add 5 min of targeted mobility work at end of session for that joint

---

## Cardio Escalation Protocol

Do not escalate cardio before you need to. Start at Phase 1 levels and move to Phase 2 levels only after:
- Week 4 deload is complete
- You've confirmed the calorie drop is working (weight trending down)

**Cardio should be the last dial you turn**, after:
1. Food tracking accuracy (dial 1)
2. Calorie targets per phase (dial 2)
3. Cardio volume increase (dial 3)

Never use cardio to compensate for poor diet adherence — it doesn't work at scale and it destroys recovery.

---

## Recovery Optimisation (Critical in Deficit)

### Sleep
- 7.5–8.5 hours is non-negotiable for recomposition
- Goa heat: use a fan or AC — sleep quality degrades above 26°C
- No screens 30 min before bed
- ZMA (zinc + magnesium) before bed improves sleep depth

### Stress
- Cortisol is the enemy of recomposition — it breaks down muscle and stores fat
- If life stress is high (work, personal), do not escalate deficit or cardio that week
- Active recovery: beach walks, swimming, yoga are all low-stress cardio alternatives

### Hydration
- Minimum 3.5–4 L water/day (Goa heat amplifies sweat losses)
- Add electrolytes if sweating heavily — pinch of Himalayan salt in water or ORS (oral rehydration salts) post-HIIT

---

## When to Abandon the Plan (Restart or Modify)

Abandon the current phase if any of these occur:
- Consistent sleep < 6 hours for more than a week
- Illness (fever, infection) — stop cardio, eat at maintenance until recovered
- Travel or life disruption for 1+ week — restart at the beginning of the current phase when you return
- Strength on all main lifts drops >10% and does not recover after 2 weeks of eating more

**After a full 12 weeks:** Take a full deload week, eat at maintenance for 2 weeks minimum, then reassess. At this point consider a muscle-building phase (lean bulk) for 8–12 weeks before another cut.

## Adherence Strategy — Habit Stacking

The biggest difference between experienced lifters succeeding or failing at recomposition is **habit systems**, not knowledge.

### Morning Routine (Lock This In First)
1. Wake → weigh yourself → log it
2. Prep breakfast while coffee brews
3. Pack Meal 2 and Meal 3 before leaving for gym
4. Train
5. Eat Meal 2 within 45 min of finishing training

### Meal Prep (Sunday — 90 min)
Prep for the week in one session:
- Grill 1.5 kg chicken breast — portion into 250g containers
- Buy and marinate fish fillets — freeze portions in zip-locks
- Cook 2 kg rice — refrigerate in portions
- Boil 15 eggs — keep in fridge
- Pre-portion almonds/cashews into small bags

This removes all in-the-moment food decisions, which is where adherence fails.

### Restaurant Strategy (Goa Specific)
You will eat out. Here are safe Goa restaurant orders:

| Option | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Grilled fish (any variety) + salad | Ask for no butter/cream sauce |
| Prawn masala (dry) + steamed rice | Estimate 1 cup rice max |
| Tandoori chicken + roti (2) | Skip the creamy dips |
| Fish curry + rice (half portion) | Coconut-based curries are moderate calorie — 1 cup max |
| Avoid: xacuti, cafreal with puri, sorpotel, vindaloo with naan | Too much hidden fat, too high calorie |

**Worst options:** Goan pork dishes, any dish with coconut cream base, anything fried in oil.

## 12-Week Timeline Summary

| Week | Key Focus | Target Actions |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Establish baselines | Log all food, test 5RM lifts, take photos |
| 2 | Lock in meal prep | Sunday prep routine, master 3 recipes |
| 3 | Strength climbing | Increase weights where hitting top of rep range |
| **4** | **DELOAD** | 40% volume, eat 2,800 kcal, sleep priority |
| 5 | Phase 2 begins | Drop to 2,700 kcal, increase cardio |
| 6 | Intensification | Add compound sets, heavier weights |
| 7 | Push hard | Maximum focus on progressive overload |
| **8** | **DELOAD** | Same as Week 4 |
| 9 | Phase 3 + PR Week | Attempt bench/squat/deadlift PRs |
| 10 | Drop sets + peak cardio | Add finishers, 80 min/week cardio |
| 11 | Second PR attempt | Push strength limits one more time |
| **12** | **DELOAD + Assessment** | Final measurements, photos, compare Week 1 |

## Post-Program Assessment (End of Week 12)

Compare:
- Week 1 photos vs Week 12 photos
- Week 1 measurements vs Week 12 measurements
- Week 1 lift numbers vs Week 12 lift numbers
- Week 1 weight vs Week 12 weight

**A successful 12 weeks = at minimum 2 of these 4 improved significantly.** Typically with good adherence you'll see all 4 move in the right direction.

After assessment, take a 2-week maintenance break (eat at TDEE, train normally, no cardio beyond walks) before planning the next phase.
