## Calorie Calculation Basis

- **BMR** (Mifflin-St Jeor, 178 cm assumed): ~2,003 kcal
- **TDEE** (×1.55 active): ~3,104 kcal → rounded to **3,100 kcal**
- Phase 1 deficit: −300 kcal → 2,800
- Phase 2 deficit: −400 kcal → 2,700
- Phase 3 deficit: −500 kcal → 2,600

## South Goa Recipes

### Recipe 1 — Goan Dry Masala Grilled Surmai (Kingfish)

**Ingredients (1 serving = 300g raw fillet):**
- 300g surmai/kingfish fillet
- 1 tsp turmeric (haldi)
- 1.5 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
- 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- 0.5 tsp cumin powder
- Salt to taste
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp coconut oil or mustard oil

**Method:**
1. Score fillet diagonally, rub marinade deep into cuts
2. Marinate 30 min minimum (overnight in fridge is best)
3. Heat non-stick pan or grill on high
4. Cook 3–4 min per side until charred on edges
5. Squeeze fresh lime, serve with onion rings and green chili

**Macros (full 300g serving):**
| Protein | Carbs | Fat | Calories |
|---------|-------|-----|----------|
| 66g | 4g | 9g | 369 |

### Recipe 2 — Prawn Sukka (South Goan Dry Masala Prawns)

**Ingredients (1 serving = 300g raw prawns shelled):**
- 300g prawns (cleaned, deveined)
- 1 medium onion (chopped)
- 1 tomato (chopped)
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp red chili
- 0.5 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- 10 curry leaves
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- Fresh coriander and lime to finish

**Method:**
1. Dry-roast spices briefly, grind with little water into paste
2. Heat coconut oil, add curry leaves + onion — cook until golden
3. Add spice paste, cook 2 min until oil separates
4. Add tomato, cook 3 min
5. Add prawns, toss to coat, cover on medium 5 min
6. Uncover, dry out excess moisture on high heat 2–3 min
7. Finish with coriander and lime

**Macros (full 300g prawn serving):**
| Protein | Carbs | Fat | Calories |
|---------|-------|-----|----------|
| 60g | 12g | 15g | 435 |

### Recipe 3 — Tandoori Chicken (Local Oven or Pan Method)

**Ingredients (1 serving = 300g raw breast):**
- 300g chicken breast (cut into large pieces, scored)
- 3 tbsp low-fat curd (dahi)
- 1 tsp red chili powder
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
- Juice of 1 lime
- Salt to taste
- 1 tsp mustard oil (in marinade only)

**Method:**
1. Mix all marinade ingredients
2. Coat chicken thoroughly — marinate minimum 2 hours, overnight preferred
3. **Pan method:** High-heat non-stick pan, cook covered 8 min, flip, cook uncovered 5 min
4. **Oven method:** 220°C for 18–20 min, broil last 3 min for char
5. Serve with sliced onion, green chutney, lime wedges

**Macros (full 300g serving):**
| Protein | Carbs | Fat | Calories |
|---------|-------|-----|----------|
| 69g | 6g | 8g | 376 |

### Recipe 4 — Goan Mackerel (Bangda) Fry

**Ingredients (2 mackerel = ~300g filleted):**
- 2 medium mackerel (cleaned, scored)
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp rice flour (for crispness — minimal, adds ~4g carbs)
- Salt + lemon
- 1 tsp coconut oil

**Method:**
1. Rub fish with all dry spices, dust with rice flour
2. Heat pan very hot, add oil
3. Cook 3 min per side until golden and crisp

**Note on mackerel:** Higher fat (13g/100g) but excellent omega-3s. Use on rest days or breakfast — the fat is beneficial for hormone production during a caloric deficit.

## Supplement Protocol

Whole food first — these fill gaps only.

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Priority |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| Creatine Monohydrate | 5g/day | Any time (consistency matters) | **High** — proven for strength + muscle retention in deficit |
| Whey Protein Isolate | 30g (1 scoop) | Post-workout if Meal 2 is delayed | **Medium** — only if hitting 240g protein from food is difficult |
| Vitamin D3 | 2,000–4,000 IU/day | Morning with fat-containing meal | **High** — most Indians are deficient; critical for testosterone |
| Omega-3 (Fish Oil) | 2–3g EPA+DHA | With any meal | **Medium** — if eating mackerel/sardines 3×/week, can skip |
| Zinc + Magnesium (ZMA) | Per label | 30 min before bed | **Low-Medium** — supports sleep quality and testosterone |
| Multivitamin | 1 daily | With breakfast | **Low** — insurance, not replacement |

> **Avoid:** Fat burners, pre-workouts with excessive stimulants, testosterone boosters. Given your cycle history, focus on natural recovery and keeping cortisol low. Excess stimulants elevate cortisol and impair recovery.

| Day Type | Rice/Chapati Serving | Carb Target |

| Heavy training (Legs, Push B) | 3 servings of rice or 4 chapatis | 240–260g |
| Moderate training (Pull A, Push A) | 2.5 servings rice or 3 chapatis | 200–220g |
| HIIT cardio day | As above + pre-HIIT banana | +20–25g |

This naturally creates the carb cycle without spreadsheets.
